Understanding Proficiency Levels

  1. Beginner

    • Limited knowledge or experience; requires guidance and supervision.
      • Understands basic concepts but struggles with practical application.
      • Relies heavily on instructions and mentoring.
      • Limited decision-making capability.
    • "Can perform basic tasks under direct supervision."

  2. Intermediate (Developing)

    • Moderate level of knowledge or experience; capable of handling routine tasks independently.
      • Can apply concepts to familiar situations with some guidance.
      • Begins to show initiative and problem-solving skills in structured settings.
      • Requires occasional support for complex or unfamiliar challenges.
    • "Handles routine tasks independently but needs assistance for advanced or complex problems."

  3. Proficient (Competent)

    • Strong knowledge or experience; able to perform independently and efficiently.
      • Confidently applies skills to both familiar and new situations.
      • Demonstrates consistency in performance and quality.
      • Occasionally seeks advice for advanced challenges but largely self-reliant.
    • "Consistently delivers high-quality work with minimal supervision."

  4. Advanced (Expert)

    • Extensive knowledge or experience; serves as a go-to person in their area of expertise.
      • Solves complex problems and provides innovative solutions.
      • Guides and mentors others; acts as a role model.
      • Demonstrates a deep understanding of both theoretical and practical aspects.
    • "Recognized as a subject matter expert and sought after for advice and guidance."
