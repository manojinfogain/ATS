CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON DISCLOSURE (NDA) AGREEMENT

This Agreement is between the Employee and NGGAWE NIRMAN TECHNOLOGIES (An Infogain Company). (hereinafter referred to as “NNT”).

In consideration of the employment with NNT, Employee agrees as follows:

1. Services

  1. While employed by NNT, Employer expects Employee to work with full devotion, sincerity and honesty and to devote his/her energy and time to his/her job at NNT. For this reason, second jobs/ part-time gainful employment in any manner whatsoever are prohibited.

  2. During the term of this agreement, and for one year after its termination, expiration, or cancellation, Employee shall not :- (i) provide or offer to provide services, other than through NNT, to any client of NNT in connection with any Engagement with respect to which Employee provided services hereunder (ii) solicit, offer or in any way, directly or indirectly, singly or in association with any other person or entity, encourage any employee, subcontractor or agent of NNT to leave NNT to work for the soliciting, offering or encouraging party.

2. Protection of NNT’s Confidential Information

a. Existence of Confidential Information

NNT owns and has developed and compiled, and will develop and compile, certain trade secrets, proprietary techniques and confidential information, which have great value to its business (referred to collectively in this Agreement as “Confidential Information”). Confidential Information includes not only information disclosed by NNT to Employee, but also information developed or learned by Employee during the course of employment with NNT. Confidential Information includes information and knowledge that was created or developed as a result of NNT’s relationship with a customer or client, and includes any confidential information of a customer or client learned by Employee during the course of employment with NNT. Confidential

Information is broadly defined, and includes all information that has or could have commercial


value or other utility in the business in which NNT is engaged or contemplates engaging, and all information of which the unauthorized disclosure could be detrimental to the interest of NNT, whether or not such information is identified as Confidential Information by NNT. By example, and without limitation, Confidential Information includes any and all information concerning trade secrets, techniques, processes, formula, computer programs, innovations, discoveries, improvements, research, data, know-how, formats, marketing plans, business plans, strategies, forecasts, unpublished financial information, budgets, projections, and customer identities, characteristics and agreements.

b. Protection of Confidential Information

Employee will not, directly or indirectly, use any Confidential Information, either during or after Employee’s employment with NNT, other than in Employee’s assigned duties and consistent with NNT’s agreement with the relevant customer or client of NNT. Employee will not, directly or indirectly, make available, sell, disclose or otherwise communicate to any third party any Confidential Information, either during or after Employee’s employment with NNT. Employee acknowledges that he or she is aware that the unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information of NNT maybe highly prejudicial to its interests, an invasion of privacy, and in improper disclosure of trade secrets.

c. Delivery of Confidential Information

Upon request or when employment with NNT terminates, Employee will immediately deliver to NNT all copies of any and all data, materials and writings received from, created for, or belonging to NNT, including, but not limited to, those which relate to or contain Confidential Information. Employee will not retain any written or tangible material (including information stored on a computer, floppies, CDs and/or any other media) containing any information concerning or disclosing any Confidential Information.

d. Secret Information Belonging to Others

Employee represents that his or her employment with NNT does not and will not

breach any agreements of the Employee or duties to a former employer or any other third defined below) to them. Employee will not disclose to NNT or use or induce NNT to use on its behalf any confidential information belonging to others. Employee party to keep confidential information belonging to others or to assign Invention Acknowledges that he or she has and will continue to abide by any confidentiality agreements reached by him/her with third parties, including the return of all materials and confidential information to those third parties.

3. Inventions

a. Assignment of Inventions

Employee hereby assigns any present or future rights he or she may have in any invention, improvement, discovery, idea, process, writing, computer program database, documentation and other original works of authorship (referred to collectively in this Agreement as “Inventions”) made or conceived by Employee solely or jointly with others which: i) is developed in whole or in part on NNT’s time or with the use of NNT’s equipment, supplies, material, facilitates or Confidential Information; ii) is suggested by or results from any task assigned to Employee or work performed by Employee for or on behalf of NNT; or iii) relates in any manner to NNT’s business, or NNT’s actual or anticipated research or development.

b. Disclosure to NNT

Employee agrees that at all times, even after the termination of employment, to promptly advise NNT of any Inventions Employee has made or conceived, either jointly or on the Employee’s own, wholly or in part during the Employee’s term of employment. NNT will hold such disclosures in confidence unless the Invention disclosed is transferred to NNT pursuant to Paragraph 3(a) hereof.

c. Prior Inventions

Except as listed in Attachment A hereto, Employee warrants that he or she presently has no right, title or interest in or to any Inventions. Employee agrees that any invention not so indicated in Attachment A at this time is subject to the provisions of paragraph 3(a) hereof.

d. Acknowledgment

Employee acknowledges that the copyright and intellectual property right in designs, computer programs, documentation, and other original works of authorship, created by the Employee (solely or jointly with others) within the scope of Employee’s employment with NNT shall always belong to NNT and shall remain NNT’s property according to the Copyright Act’ 1957.

e. Moral Rights

With regard to any of the Inventions assigned by paragraph 3 (a), NNT and it subsidiaries, licensees (direct and indirect) are not required to designate Employee as author of any Inventions, of which NNT is always



deemed to be the sole author, when distributed publicly or otherwise. Employee waives and releases, to the extent permitted by law, all rights to the foregoing. In addition, Employee hereby transfers and assigns any “moral” rights to the extent such transfer or assignment is permitted by applicable law that Employee may have in any such Invention under any copyright or other similar or dissimilar law. Employee agrees to waive and never to assert any such “moral” rights in such Inventions during or after the termination of employment with NNT.


Employee also acknowledges that NNT, its subsidiaries and assigns have the unfettered right to adapt, change, modify, add to, subtract from and otherwise alter or deal with any such Invention which Employee authored, co-authored, invented or co-invented, in whatever way NNT chooses and that NNT may grant other such right. The Employee once again repeats and reiterates that NNT shall always be a deemed author.

g. Assistance to NNT

Employee agrees that at all times during and after Employee’s employment with NNT, the Employee will do whatever NNT requests Employee to assign any Inventions to NNT or to assist NNT in filing patent applications or otherwise gaining or securing title, right to use, copyrights, patents or the like for Inventions described in Paragraph 3(a) hereof.


h. Injunctive Relief

Employee acknowledges that a violation of this paragraph of the Agreement will constitute immediate and irreparable damage to NNT, which cannot be fully and adequately compensated in money and which will warrant preliminary and other injunctive relief, an order for specific performance and other equitable relief, in addition to any relief or remedies to which NNT is entitled.

4. Standards of Conduct

NNT expects its employees to maintain the highest standards of business conduct, and to be totally honest and ethical in all of NNT’s business dealings. NNT always conducts its business in accordance will applicable rules and laws, and expects its employees to do the same.
