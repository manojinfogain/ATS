Prevention of Sexual Harassment Policy

1. Objective:

Infogain is committed to providing a place of work free of sexual harassment, intimidation or exploitation. To ensure an environment free from sexual harassment as well as harassment on account of an individual’s race, religious creed, color, national origin, citizenship, disability, marital status, age or sex. It is expected that all employees will treat one another and visitors of Infogain with respect.


2. Scope:

The policy will cover:

  1. All Infogain employees,
  2. Customers, Subcontractors, Suppliers and Guests on Infogain’s premises.

3. Definition Of Sexual Harassment:

As per ‘The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal ) Act 2013- No woman employee at a work place shall be subjected to sexual harassment including unwelcome sexually determined behavior, physical contact, advances, sexually colored remarks, showing pornography, sexual demand, epithet, slur, making unsolicited invasive inquiries into private lives of employees, asking person’s sexual fantasy, consensual relationship, request for sexual favors or any other unwelcome conduct of sexual nature whether verbal, physical, graphic, or electronic or by any other actions, which may include:-


  1. implied or overt promise of preferential treatment in employment; or
  2. implied or overt threat of detrimental treatment in employment; or
  3. implied or overt threat about the present of future employment status;
  4. conduct which interferes with work or creates an intimidating or offensive or hostile work environment; or
  5. humiliating conduct constituting health and safety problems.

    6. The followings would also constitute sexual harassment:


  6. Submission of unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favours, and verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature.
    When unwelcome sexual advances, and verbal, non-verbal and/or physical conduct such as loaded comments, remarks or jokes, letters, phone calls or e-mails, gestures, exhibition of pornography, lurid stares, physical contact, stalking, touching, patting, pinching, fondling, assaulting, brushing against other's body, sounds or display of a derogatory nature have the purpose and/or effect of interfering with an individual's performance or of creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive environment.
  7. When a person/s uses, with a sexual purpose, the body or any part of it or any object as an extension of the body in relation to another person without the latter's consent or against that person's will, such conduct will amount to sexual assault.
  8. When deprecatory comments, conduct or any such behavior is based on the gender identity/sexual orientation of the person or when hostile environment is created on the basis of a person’s gender identity/sexual orientation.
  9. The sexual harassment may occur irrespective of genders and will include both male & female.

4. Infogain Premises:

Defined as all land, offices, building, lodging, quarters, equipment’s, vehicles and parking areas under the control of Infogain. Additionally included, as Infogain premises are business trips, business related social events, such other work locations as the job site of a customer, supplier or subcontractor, including the transportation vehicles occupied during the travel to and from those locations while on company business, or any location where Infogain business is transacted.

6. Procedure For Filing Complaint

To complain for such incidence, the aggrieved employee may complain in writing through e-mail to Internal Complaint Committee at posh@infogain.com
The complaint may also be written individually to the Chairperson/ any member of the Committee/Superior Officer i.e. HR Head, HR Manager, Practice Director/President or Chairman/ respective HR Business Partners who would forward the same to Internal Complaint Committee. Where complaint is made to a person other than the committee, the concerned person will forward the same to the complaint committee. All these complaints will be attended with due speed and promptness. Immediate reporting helps in investigation. In case the woman has filed a verbal complain, the committee shall render all reasonable assistance to reduce the same in writing. If it cannot be reported immediately, the complaint should be registered no less than 3 months from the date of incident / date of last incident occurred. Beyond that it will be treated as time barred, basis the severity of the situation and circumstances not prevented the employee to file a complaint.


7. Investigation and Award of Punishment


7.1 Conciliation

On the receipt of the complaint and request of the aggrieved woman the committee, as the case may be, before initiating enquiry, takes steps to settle the matter between her and the respondent through conciliation. When the settlement is arrived at, the committee shall record the settlement and recommend the management not to take any action in the matter. The copies of the settlement will be sent to the aggrieved party and the respondent. Also for such cases no inquiry shall be conducted by the committee here on.


7.2 Investigation when conciliation is not arrived at

The committee shall proceed ahead with the investigation/inquiry.

  • Once a complaint is lodged the identity of complainant, aggrieved party and respondent will be kept strictly confidential.
  • Contact between both the parties should be reduced.
  • The investigation may include but not limited to summoning & enforcing the attendance of any person and examining him / her, witness examination, facts presentation etc.
  • Internal complaint committee will coordinate with a third party (NGO representative) and symbiotically investigate the matter to reach a conclusion.
  • The investigation shall be completed within a period of 40- 60 days.
  • On completion of investigation the ICC shall provide a report of its findings to management within a period of 10 days from the date of completion of inquiry and such report shall be made available to the concerned parties.

Retaliation

Retaliation against an employee who has complained about sexual harassment or any individual who has cooperated in investigation or friends or family is strictly prohibited and will not be tolerated by Infogain.


7.3 Other Points to be considered

  • Where the committee arrives at the conclusion that no satisfactory outcome can be drawn, the case should be forwarded under legal jurisdiction / to police as applicable.
  • In case the committee finds the degree of offence coverable under the Indian Penal code, then this fact shall be mentioned in the report and appropriate action shall be initiated to make a police complaint within 7 days of the incident reported.

7.4 Penalty/Punishment

  • Where the committee arrives at the conclusion that the allegations against the respondent have not been proved, it shall recommend to the CEO/Chairman/President not to take any action.
  • Where the committee arrives at the conclusion that the allegation against the respondent has been proved, it shall recommend to the CEO/Chairman/President as the case may be. The committee may recommend, depending on the severity of the case, to take action for misconduct in accordance with the terms and condition of service rules of appointment applicable to the respondent which may include but not limited to:
  • Counselling, verbal / written warning, job transfers / suspension, termination and legal actions as applicable.
  • Monetary compensation to aggrieved party in case charges are proved (can be deducted from salary by employer) would include:
    • Mental trauma, pain, suffering and emotional distress caused
    • Loss in career opportunity
    • Medical expenses occurred – Physical/ psychiatric treatment
  • Imprisonment may range within 1-3 years/fine/ both.

Also, in case the allegations against the respondent is found false or malicious then strict action would be taken against such person to file the complaint up to and including to termination.


8. Documentation

  • In event of any complaint lodged the case should be documented separately with complete and accurate documentation of complaints, investigation procedure adopted, case findings and resolution thereof and submitted to HR department.
  • Apart from maintaining the separate files related to the cases, records of disciplinary action should be

kept in personal files of proven guilty and can be disclosed as required by law.

  • Documents pertaining to the case to be maintained by Human Resource Department in strict confidence.

Note – Sexual Harassment complaints closed by committee once recommended actions are presented to management and implemented through Human Resources.


9. Other Points to be considered
To communicate and create awareness of the provisions of this policy to all employees.

Which would include:

  • Special training sessions for all the employees
  • Enable them to understand the meaning of sexual and other prohibited harassment;
  • Ensure that they are able to identify such harassment;
  • Ensure that they understand that their own conduct may be a violation of the policy and would constitute serious misconduct;
  • Enable them to understand how to prevent conduct directed toward them, or in their work environment, which they find unwelcome.

10. Responsibility of Human Resource Department

  • To ensure that all employees are made aware of the provisions of the policy.
  • To ensure that the new hires as well existing employees have clearly understood the policy.
  • To ensure that a duly signed copy of the certificate stating that they are aware of the policy forms the part of their personal dossier.
  • Quarterly sessions highlighting the same are conducted periodically.
  • Display policy at all prominent places in the organization.
  • To ensure that the police verification of the cab drivers or any staff escorting the ladies in vehicle is done.
  • The other staff members like office boys, assistants are made aware of the provision of the policy.

11. Responsibilities of the employees at all levels

  • To understand and comprehend the company’s policy on sexual harassment.
  • To maintain his/her personal conduct relative to the provisions of this policy. Because sexual harassment may consist of physical, verbal or visual behavior, all employees are advised to consult Head Human Resources for guidance if they have any questions or concerns about issues related to workplace conduct or behavior.
    “I never meant anything by this” will not be considered as valid defense against harassment.

This policy supercedes all earlier policies, notification in this regard and the revised policy takes effect immediately.

ANNEXURE - I


Guidelines for the Committee


  1. All complaints to the committee should be in writing. Incase the complaint is verbal the same should be reduced in writing.

  2. Any complaint should be forwarded to the Chairperson of the complaint committee.

  3. The committee will examine the complaint meet the respondent and the complainer in an effort to arrive at conciliation.

  4. Incase the conciliation is not arrived at then the committee would proceed ahead with the investigation.

  5. Once the complaint is received the committee will call for all the witness and collect the evidence related to the complaint.

  6. The statements of all the witness in support of the complaint would be reduced in writing duly signed by the witnesses.

  7. The respondent would also be called and evidence in his support would also be collected along with the statements of the witness.

  8. Once this is done then the date and time of the first hearing is fixed.

  9. Once the investigation is done and the allegations against the respondent are proved the committee would submit the written report of the same to the CEO/Chairman/President of the organization. The quantum punishment would be decided by the committee on case to case basis.

  10. The whole process of investigation should be completed in 40-60 days.

  11. Basis the severity of offence committed the committee may define the recourse mechanism as reconciliation, proceeding further with inquiry, disciplinary actions and legal actions /police complaints.

  12. At any given point of time as required under judicial parameters the information may be disclosed to court.
