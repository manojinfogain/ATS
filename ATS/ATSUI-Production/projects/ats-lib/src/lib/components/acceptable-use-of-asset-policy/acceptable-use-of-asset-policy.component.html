1. PURPOSE

The purpose of this policy is to make employees and external party users aware of the rules for the acceptable use of assets associated with information and information processing. Guiding principles, individual responsibility, intellectual property, use of personal equipment, internet and email usage, instant messaging, social media, clear desk / clear screen, and working offsite are covered in this policy.

2. SCOPE

This document provides guidelines for all employees to use the assets prudently that are associated with information and information processing.

3. INTENDED AUDIENCE

This policy applies to all Infogain employees including third parties, who manage or have responsibility for systems or data stored on systems within Infogain.

4. STATEMENT OF POLICY

Intentions for publishing an Acceptable Use Policy are not to impose restrictions that are contrary to Infogain's established culture of openness, trust, and integrity. ISS department is committed to protecting Infogain employees, partners, and the company from illegal or damaging actions by individuals, either knowingly or unknowingly.

5. ACCEPTABLE USE OF ASSETS

5.1. General Use and Ownership

  • Infogain shall be the sole owner of any information stored in company’s information processing facilities and its protection shall be ensured by the users.

  • Theft, loss, or unauthorized disclosure of company’s proprietary information shall be promptly reported to ISS/CISO Function/Admin department.

  • For security and network maintenance purposes, authorized system administrators of the company shall always monitor equipment, systems and network traffic.

  • Infogain reserves the right to audit networks and systems on a periodic basis to ensure compliance with policy.

  • User’s PC/laptop shall be protected from unauthorized access by locking screen before leaving the system unattended.

  • User shall log-off from applications or network services, when no longer required.

  • User shall be solely responsible for all actions conducted using his/her user id and sharing of passwords/RSA tokens.

  • Only licensed and authorized software shall be installed on the user’s desktop/laptop.

  • Computer printout of confidential documents shall be kept in a secure place and shall be shredded before throwing into dustbin, if not useful.

  • User shall take utmost care while sharing data with other users in network to ensure that right permission is given to right users.

  • User shall not share confidential documents in network.

  • User shall use strong/complex password to protect all sensitive business documents/individual files stored on Laptops/desktops.

  • User shall not reveal account password to others or allow use of their account /asset by others. This includes family and other household members when work is being done at home.

  • User shall use an official laptop for business purposes only and shall not be used by family or friends.

  • User shall use extreme caution when opening e-mail attachments received from unknown senders, which may contain malware.

  • User shall delete suspicious/unsolicited e-mails immediately. Continuous receipt of such emails shall be reported to the Service Desk of ISS and CISO Function.

  • EMAIL and Internet facilities of the company shall be used as per Infogain’s Electronic Communication policy.

  • User shall ask ISS department to do physical movement of PC or any other hardware equipment.

  • User shall not install/uninstall any software on/from the organization’s computer without the prior approval from ISS department and CISO Function.

  • Use of pirated software is absolutely prohibited on any computer system of the organization and the media containing such software shall not be brought inside the organization’s premises.

  • User shall not share any official data/information to internal/external users for non-businessrelated purpose.

  • User shall not make any changes to the hardware configuration of the PC.

  • Under no circumstances user shall not be engaged in any activity that is illegal under local or national laws while utilizing Infogain-owned/leased IT and Communication resources.

  • Introduction of or experimenting with malicious programs into the network, desktop or server (e.g., viruses, worms, Trojan horses, e-mail bombs, etc.) is strictly prohibited.

  • Making fraudulent offers of products, items, or services originating from any Infogain account is strictly prohibited. Create, circulate, forward, distribute, store and/or download any material or make any statements:

    • Which can cause offence to any person to whom it is addressed?

    • Which can be considered to infringe the Company's Equal Opportunities Policy or be in any way discriminating or harassing (whether sexually, racially, or otherwise).

    • Statements of a pornographic, sexual, or obscene nature, defamatory.

    • Which may result in financial or legal liability, or which may damage the reputation of the Company, are highly prohibited using official assets. Any such instance will lead to necessary disciplinary action against the employee.

5.2.Internet Usage

5.2.1. Accessing the internet

  • Users are provided access to the Internet to assist them in the performance of their jobs. At any time, at the request of management, Internet access may be revoked. IT may restrict access to certain Internet sites that reduce network performance or are known or found to be compromised with and by malware. INFOGAIN will use internet filters to block high-risk content and deny access to any unwanted material or malware in support of the Acceptable Use Policy.

  • All software used to access the Internet must be part of the INFOGAIN standard software suite or approved by IT. Such software must incorporate all vendor provided security patches.

  • Users accessing the Internet through a computer connected to INFOGAIN’s network must do so through an approved Internet firewall or other security device. All software used to access the Internet shall be configured to use a proxy or other means of managing or controlling. Bypassing INFOGAIN’s network security, by accessing the Internet directly, is strictly prohibited.

  • Users are prohibited from using INFOGAIN Internet access for unauthorized access to local and remote computer systems, software piracy, illegal activities, the transmission of threatening, obscene, or harassing materials, or personal solicitations. All computer devices connected to INFOGAIN's network must have security software like antivirus, anti-malware etc. installed on them while connecting to the internet/network.

  • User shall not use/access online storage accounts to store official or personal information.

  • User shall not use Internet Proxy Server other than provided by the organization as it is prohibited.

  • User shall not download and share Mobile Codes and publish codes to any public forum/unknown sources as it is considered a security violation.

  • User shall not misuse, disclose and alter customer or personnel information without authorization.

  • User shall not deliberately point or hyper-link of company web sites to other Internet/WWW sites whose content may be inconsistent with or in violation of the aims or policies of the company.

  • Unauthorized copying of copyrighted material including, but not limited to, digitization and distribution of photographs from magazines, books or other copyrighted sources, copyrighted music, and the installation of any copyrighted S/W for which Infogain or the end user does not have an active license is strictly prohibited. User shall not use Internet for illegal activity, to access illegal materials, or to access materials that are obscene/ pornographic/ sexually explicit material.

  • Port scanning or security scanning is expressly prohibited unless prior notification to system administration is made and approved.

  • Executing any form of network monitoring which will intercept data not intended for the employee's office network unless this activity is a part of the employee's normal job/duty.

  • Using any program/script/command, or sending messages of any kind, with the intent to interfere with, or disable, a user's terminal session, via any means, locally or via the Internet/Intranet/Extranet is not allowed.

  • User shall not carry personal mass storage devices in office premises.

5.2.2. Expectation of privacy

  • Users should have no expectation of privacy in anything they create, store, send, or receive using INFOGAIN’s Internet access.

  • Users expressly waive any right of privacy in anything they create, store, send, or receive using INFOGAIN’s Internet access.

5.2.3. File downloads and virus protection

  • Users are prohibited from downloading and installing software on their PC without proper authorization from ISS. Technical controls may be utilized to limit the download and installation of software.

  • Downloaded software may be used only in ways that conform to its license and copyrights.

  • All files downloaded from the Internet must be scanned for viruses using INFOGAIN approved virus detection software. If a user suspects a file may be infected, he/she must notify ISS immediately.

  • Users are prohibited from using the Internet to deliberately propagate any virus, worm, Trojan Horse, trapdoor, or other malicious program.

5.2.4. Monitoring of computer and internet usage

  • All user activity on INFOGAIN IT assets is subject to logging and review. INFOGAIN has the right to monitor and log all aspects of its systems including, but not limited to, monitoring Internet sites visited by users, monitoring chat and newsgroups, monitoring file downloads, and all communications sent and received by users.

5.2.5. Frivolous use

  • Computer resources are not unlimited. Network bandwidth and storage capacity have finite limits, and all users connected to the network have a responsibility to conserve these resources. As such, the user must not deliberately perform acts that waste computer resources or unfairly monopolize resources to the exclusion of others. These acts include, but are not limited to, spending excessive amounts of time on the Internet, playing games, engaging in online chat groups, uploading, or downloading large files, accessing streaming audio and/or video files, or otherwise creating unnecessary loads on network traffic associated with nonbusiness-related uses of the Internet. Personal use, beyond incidental use of the Internet, may be done only on break room PCs and only in compliance with this policy.

5.3.Laptop Security and Usage

5.3.1. Laptop Usage Policy & Declaration

  • Prior to handing over the laptop to the user, user needs to read & sign/acknowledge the Laptop security Declaration cum undertaking form duly signed by ISS ISSUING AUTHORITY. This shall be applicable for both Infogain provided, and client provided laptops.

  • Infogain laptops are provided to all Infogain employees.

  • Client provided laptops are being assigned to the respective employees and maintained the list for the same by respective PM, except few specific clients as applicable.

  • User shall have prior approval from client to take laptop at home for work.

  • User shall regularly update the virus scan definitions.

  • Employee shall not bring personal laptop in the office.

  • User shall not install any potential malware software/firmware capable of exposing official data in devices used by the user.

5.3.2. Physical security guidelines for laptop

  • Asset list of all company and client provided laptops (which are assigned through ISS) shall be maintained by ISS department.

  • Laptops shall not be left unattended in public places. Keep your laptop close and be particularly vigilant when passing through airport security.

  • Personal laptops are strictly prohibited in the premises.

  • In addition to the above guidelines utmost care should be taken while handling the client provided laptops.

  • Laptop users shall always carry laptops with them before leaving the office. Leaving the laptops in the employee’s personal drawers or anywhere else is strictly prohibited, as an exception, only in case as required by the client, laptops are kept inside the drawer with proper lock & key.

  • The employee shall be fully accountable for theft, loss or damage of the property.

  • The employee shall be liable to replace or pay an amount of Rs. 10,000 to the organization in case of theft, loss, or damage to the property. Employee’s Immediate Reporting Manager, the practice Director & the president shall also be liable to pay Rs. 5000 each in such incidents. The organization retains the right to deduct the same from the salary in case of such an event.

  • The Incident can occur in two ways: -

    • Laptop stolen from office premises

    • Laptop stolen outside the premises

    Laptop Stolen from office premises

    If the laptop is stolen from office premises following steps shall be taken:

    • Employee shall inform their respective PM, ISS, CISO Function, HR and Admin department. Accordingly respective employee/PM/RM shall file it as a Security Incident on CISO Function Infogain service helpdesk.

    • The immediate F.I.R shall be done by the employee within 24 hours.

    • The Security spocs from Administration, HR, ISS and CISO Function will investigate by first viewing the CCTV footage. (CCTV is installed at various entry & exit points and passage area).

    • The detailed questionnaire session will be done with the respective employee to identify series of events and conduct Root Cause Analysis on the incident.

    • Infogain has the right to ask questions from any suspected employee/support staff as per the CCTV footage and employees questionnaire session. If after investigation anybody found guilty a disciplinary action will be taken by the management, up to and including termination of employment.

    Laptop Stolen Outside office premises:

    If the laptop is stolen outside the office premises, the employee shall immediately file F.I.R and inform their respective PM, ISS, CISO Function, and Admin department within 24 hours. Information security incident should also be raised on CISO Function Infogain service helpdesk accordingly. The F.I.R copy to be submitted to the ISS, CISO Function, Admin & Finance department as a proof.

5.4.Smart Phones/Tablet Usage

  • Users shall use only official smart phone/tablet for accessing company’s information processing facilities.

  • User shall not connect these devices to a PC/Laptop without having up-to-date malware protection software.

  • Users shall be cautious about the merging of personal and official email accounts on their devices. All official communication shall be done through official email system only while using these devices.

  • User shall not be allowed to use “jailbreak” (iPhone) or install any software/firmware on company/client provided phones/tablet.

  • User shall not attempt to connect their smart phones intentionally/unintentionally to their systems and transfer any kind of official data.

5.5.Electronic Communication

To adhere to the electronic communication policy of Infogain :

Employee shall:

  • Utilize only those forms of electronic communication resources for which they are authorized.

  • Get approval of the owner of the email group first to send mail to an entire distribution list.

  • Use Infogain Email for business purpose only.

  • Use an appropriate subject line that conveys the clear message about the mail content.

  • If user receives an unexpected mail (Phishing email) from external domain, he/she has to report it to ISS/CISO Function.

Employee shall not:

  • Provide access to any authorized or unauthorized users by sharing logon ids and Passwords as it is strictly prohibited.

  • Access information by any unauthorized means, such as imitating another system deliberately, impersonating another user or other person or misuse of legal user credentials (usernames, passwords, etc.

  • Transmit copyrighted software or information through the Company’s electronic systems.

  • Copy information originated by others and re-post it without acknowledging the original source, even if the content is modified.

  • Send or forward emails containing libelous, defamatory, offensive, racist or obscene remarks. If you receive an e-mail of this nature, you must promptly notify your supervisor.

  • Users shall use only official smart phone/tablet for accessing company’s information processing facilities.

  • User shall not connect these devices to a PC/Laptop without having up-to-date malware protection software.

5.5.1. Email Etiquettes

Employee shall:

  • Be formal.

  • Keep messages brief and to the point.

  • Use sentence case while sending/replying to email.

  • Use BCC to send messages to a large distribution list as recipients won't have to see a huge list of names.

  • Use the subject field to indicate content and purpose.

  • Use a signature at the end of message that includes contact information.

  • Summarize long discussions.

  • Avoid using emoticons, if required then use sparingly so that it doesn't appear unprofessional.

  • Mail on the below mentioned department Email-IDs only, in case of grievance related to any particular department.

Users shall not be allowed to share Company / Client confidential information to external domains.

5.5.2. E-mail Privacy

  • At any time without prior notice, the Company reserves the right to examine any/all email, files, folders, or other information stored on media belonging to the Company. Users shall have no expectations of privacy in this regard. Infogain management may disclose contents of e-mail either internally or to external parties, where necessary, for a legitimate business reason, without further permission of the associate. Infogain management will reserve the right to inspect and review any data maintained in its e-mail system without prior consent of, or notification to, the associate.

5.6.Social Media Usage

5.6.1. Terms and conditions of use

  • All requests for a INFOGAIN Official’s use of external social media, on behalf of INFOGAIN, must be submitted to the Senior Management Team. INFOGAIN may allow access to select pre-approved social media websites. INFOGAIN Officials may only access these sites in a manner consistent with INFOGAIN’s security protocols and INFOGAIN Officials may not circumvent ISS Security protocols to access social media sites.

  • If an INFOGAIN employee/associate/official becomes aware of any possible confidential information disclosure on any of social media platforms, then they shall report the same to CISO immediately.

  • The passwords for the INFOGAIN official social media accounts should be complex and based on secure password policy. The passwords should be changed at a regular or pre-defined time interval.

  • Use of personal social media accounts and user IDs, for INFOGAIN use, is prohibited.

  • Use of INFOGAIN social media user IDs, for personal use, is prohibited. Use of INFOGAIN email addresses to register on social networks, blogs, or other online tools utilized for personal use is prohibited. Examples of prohibited use of company User IDs include:

  • Downloading and installing plug-ins or helper applications such as those that try to access the INFOGAIN e-mail directory

  • Joining groups using a company user ID for personal reasons

  • Adding personal friends to a INFOGAIN Official’s friends list

  • INFOGAIN Officials are to acknowledge they have reviewed the social media service’s Terms of Service (TOS) or Terms of User (TOU), as applicable. Links for sites are below

Facebook: https://www.facebook.com/terms.php

LinkedIn: http://www.linkedin.com/static?key=user_agreement

Twitter: http://twitter.com/tos

YouTube: http://www.youtube.com/t/terms

5.6.2. Representing Infogain

  • INFOGAIN Senior Management will designate a person or team to manage and respond to social media issues concerning INFOGAIN and will determine who will have the authority to contribute content. This person(s)’s responsibilities will include, but are not limited to:

    • Managing social media tools and channels.

    • Responding to questions internally and externally about social media sites.

    • Addressing problems/providing direction for staff if a user becomes threatening, abusive, or harassing.

    • Suggesting changes to this INFOGAIN social media policy when warranted.

    • Working with other staff to make sure opportunities aren’t overlooked in marketing INFOGAIN services; and

    • Training staff to ensure they understand how to use INFOGAIN’s social media program.

  • INFOGAIN will take the necessary steps to make sure the content complies with applicable laws and regulations.

  • All INFOGAIN Officials who participate in social media, on behalf of INFOGAIN, are expected to represent INFOGAIN in a professional manner. Failure to do so could have a negative impact on INFOGAIN and could jeopardize INFOGAIN Official’s ability to participate in social media in the future.

  • INFOGAIN owns all authorized social media and networking content. INFOGAIN Officials are prohibited from taking, saving, or sending any INFOGAIN content distributed via social media while employed, separated, serving on the Board of Directors, or terminated by INFOGAIN.

  • New technologies and social networking tools continually evolve. As new tools emerge, this policy will be updated to reflect the changes.

  • Platforms for online collaboration are fundamentally changing the work environment and offering new ways to engage with members and the community. Guiding principles for participating in social media should be followed.

    • Post meaningful, respectful comments and refrain from remarks that are off-topic or offensive.

    • Reply to comments quickly when a response is appropriate.

    • Know and follow the state and federal laws that always protect member confidentiality.

    • Protect proprietary information and confidentiality.

    • When disagreeing with others’ opinions, keep it professional.

    • Know the INFOGAIN Code of Conduct and apply the standards and principles in social computing.

5.6.3. Personal blogs and posts

  • INFOGAIN takes no position on a INFOGAIN Official ‘s decision to start or maintain a blog or personal website or to participate in other online social media activities outside of work. INFOGAIN Officials, identifying themselves as a INFOGAIN Official on a social network, should ensure their profile and related content is consistent with how they and INFOGAIN wish for them to present themselves. This includes what the INFOGAIN Official writes about himself/herself and the type of photos he/she publishes.

  • INFOGAIN Officials must not reveal proprietary information and must be cautious about posting exaggerations, obscenities, or other characterizations that could invite litigation.

  • INFOGAIN Officials must not make public reference to any INFOGAIN related cash or security procedures.

  • INFOGAIN Officials who comment on any INFOGAIN business or policy issue must clearly identify themselves as a INFOGAIN Official in their blog or posting and include a disclaimer that the views are their own and not those of INFOGAIN. When generating content that deals with INFOGAIN or individuals associated with INFOGAIN, INFOGAIN Officials should use a disclaimer such as “The postings on this site are my own and do not necessarily reflect the views of INFOGAIN”.

  • INFOGAIN Officials must not use social media websites to harass, threaten, discriminate against, disparage, or defame any other INFOGAIN Officials, members, vendors, INFOGAIN products, services, or business philosophy.

  • INFOGAIN Officials are prohibited from disclosing confidential, proprietary, or otherwise sensitive business or personal information related to INFOGAIN or any of its INFOGAIN Officials, vendors, or members. INFOGAIN Officials are also prohibited from disclosing any confidential, proprietary, or otherwise sensitive business or personal information that could identify another INFOGAIN Official, vendor, or member without that individual’s prior authorization.

  • INFOGAIN Officials should not take any action via social media websites or personal blogs that would harm, or is likely to harm, the reputation of INFOGAIN or any INFOGAIN Officials, members, or vendors.

5.7.Software Usage

ISS Team is owner of overall management of software licenses and shall install all permitted/approved software based on their license availability only after approval from PM/CISO/Director. Infogain employees are assigned computer systems to be used for Infogain & client’s business requirements, will agree to the following terms and conditions for the use of software:

  • All software must be purchased or acquired through the Company’s Infrastructure Systems Support (ISS) Department, including authorization, and obtaining the necessary licenses for such software. This practice will also ensure software purchased is on the Company's list of supported software, purchased only from reputable, authorized resellers or direct from the manufacturer, and includes original user material such as documentation and license agreements.

  • The installation of software, including client supplied, free and/or open-source software, without the approval and assistance of the ISS Department is prohibited. Most of this software (even the free versions) are made available based on licensing conditions which allow the product company to audit the software user’s organization for software usage, therefore the downloading and installation of software via the Internet is strictly prohibited without the approval and assistance of the Company’s ISS Department.

  • Software obtained via the Internet can contain viruses, therefore the downloading and installation of software via the Internet is strictly prohibited without the approval and assistance of the Company’s ISS Department. The downloading of any software, material, music, movies, and other copyrighted content from Internet site are strictly prohibited.

  • Employees are prohibited from installing any games, utilities, screen savers, wallpaper, or other personal software on Infogain provided laptop or desktop.

  • According to applicable copyright law and Infogain Zero Tolerance Security Policy, persons involved in the illegal use or reproduction of software can be subject to strict disciplinary action including civil damages/ criminal penalties/ imprisonment and warning/ suspension/ termination respectively. The Company does not condone the illegal duplication of software. Employees shall not make, acquire, or use unauthorized copies of computer software.

  • All software use should be limited to official Company business.

  • The Company will conduct software audit, on random basis, as a part of ISMS audit. The purpose of such an audit is to ensure software license compliance by employees. Full cooperation of all employees is required during such audits.

To ensure proper support levels, ensure compatibility with other applications, minimize potential software conflicts and plan for future collaborative efforts the Company had set forth certain software standards. Such software can be made available to all employees based on appropriate manager or budgetary approval (If needed).

5.8.Clear Desk and Clear Screen

As part of clear desk and clear screen policy, user should take care of the below mentioned guidelines:

  • Lock away all sensitive and confidential information in cabinets or pedestals when the desk is unattended for an extended period. For e.g. when away for meetings, at lunchtimes or overnight.

  • Clean your workspace at the end of each workday.

  • Log off your workstations when unattended.

  • Sensitive or critical business information, e.g. on paper or on electronic storage media, is locked away when not required, especially when the office is vacated.

  • All documents containing sensitive or classified information printed on dedicated or shared printers should be collected immediately.

  • No sensitive information shall be lying scattered on tables or other places (fax machine, photocopy area, etc.). In case the documents are found unattended, the same is shredded by the security personnel.

  • All disposal of sensitive information (include proposals, all confidential documents, pricing information, salary information, operations performance, and other strategic information, etc.) should be done by shredding the documents.

  • Use of recycling bins for sensitive documents when no longer needed.

7. Privacy

The organization can monitor the use of its IT systems and the data on it at any time. This may include (except where precluded by local privacy laws) examination of the content stored within the email and data files of any user, and examination of the access history of any users.

The organization reserves the right to regularly audit networks and systems to ensure compliance with this policy.

The organization shall, in conducting such monitoring activities, use all reasonable endeavors to comply with regulatory guidelines and to respect its employee’s privacy.

8. Enforcement

Any employee found violating this policy shall be subject to disciplinary action including termination of employment.
